Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Known as the "Missile Man" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

He was also a beloved teacher, author, and visionary, who inspired millions of youth with his life, ideas, and humility. Dr. Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, and passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Throughout his career, Kalam remained closely associated with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
